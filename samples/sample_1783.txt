US expels Cuban diplomats over ‘health attacks’ The United States has expelled 15 Cuban diplomats and ordered the shuttering of the country’s consulate in the US city of Seattle, in retaliation for the alleged sonic attacks against its personnel in Havana, officials said Thursday. “The United States took these actions to protect our personnel,” State Department spokeswoman Heather Nauert said in a statement. “The decision was made due to Cuba’s failure to take appropriate steps to protect our diplomats in accordance with its obligations under the Vienna Convention,” she added. Nauert said the US had not been able to determine “which individuals or entities were responsible for these attacks.” But she said the move was “not a definitive response to the attacks,” suggesting more actions could be taken against Cuba.
