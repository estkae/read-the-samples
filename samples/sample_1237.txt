To mark its 45th anniversary, Jaguar is reintroducing the D-Type, the “grail” of all racing cars, into the Jaguar Heritage Portfolio. The D-Type has gone through many restoration phases since the brand first acquired it in 1954. The car is currently on loan to the National Motor Museum, Beaulieu, England, but was once one of the eight short-wheelbase cars driven by legendary drivers such as Sir Stirling Moss and Mike Hawthorn in 1956.\n\nRead Full Article
